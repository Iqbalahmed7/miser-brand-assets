# 01 — Foundation

## The name

**Miser.** A company whose job is counting your money and spending as little
of it as possible. The name is the product description.

## The spirit

An accountant with good taste. A bouncer at the wallet. Someone who takes
other people's money seriously because it isn't theirs. Not the cackling
cartoon miser — the quiet, disciplined one.

## Positioning

> *A quiet defence of the small number.*

Miser is the cost layer for AI coding. It sits between your agent and the
API (or alongside it) and makes careful, continuous decisions: which model,
which cache, which context to carry, which retries to stop. It reports the
arithmetic in full.

## Strategic principle: reclamation, but sober

The name Miser carries cultural weight. In English-speaking markets there is
a reclaimable wink (like *Boring Company*). In South Asian, Arab, East Asian,
and parts of Latin American markets, "miser" is a genuine insult with no
reclaimable edge.

**Our decision:** treat the word seriously, not ironically. The brand never
winks, never says "yes we're cheap lol." It frames *miserliness* as
discipline, not a character flaw. *"Small numbers, taken seriously"* reads
the same in London and Mumbai.

This is the guardrail: we defend the name by making the work admirable, not
by leaning on reclamation humour.

## Brand principles

1. **Restraint over volume.** If a page has a shouting match and a whispered
   aside, Miser is the aside.
2. **Editorial, not promotional.** Copy reads like a well-edited broadsheet,
   not a landing page.
3. **Arithmetic over narrative.** When in doubt, show the number.
4. **Dry, not cold.** We're serious, not humourless. A single wry aside per
   page, tucked into italics, is enough.
5. **Universal legibility.** Works in English, works translated, works at
   16px, works on paper.

## What Miser is not

- Loud, neon, Silicon-Valley-confident
- Ironic, winking, meme-literate
- Preachy, austere, monastic-solemn
- Dependent on a mascot, character, or illustration style
